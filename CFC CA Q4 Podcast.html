New Podcast Episode on Strategies to Prevent Falls and Promote Independence in Older Adults
Falls are the leading cause of injury among older Canadians, with 20-30% seniors experiencing one or more falls each year. What many people don’t realise is that falls are not an inevitable part of ageing; they are often predictable and preventable with the right strategies.
In our latest ComForConnections episode, we explore how evidence-based balance training and fall-prevention approaches can slow symptom progression, reduce fall risk, and promote safe, confident ageing. Experts discuss the key physical and environmental factors that contribute to falls, as well as practical ways to support safe movement and independence at home, including how ComForCare programs and trusted partner initiatives help build strength, mobility, and long-term wellness for older adults ageing in place.
Get ready to unpack the factors behind falls and share proven strategies that support strength, balance, and independence at home, from evidence-based exercise programs to practical home safety tips.

Learn more about:

  • The prevalence of falls in Canada and their impact on older adults’ health, independence, mobility, and long-term quality of life.
  • Practical strategies to consider safe movement, meaningful activity, and daily independence for older adults at home.
  • Actionable insights for ageing services professionals on implementing evidence-based fall prevention and risk-mitigation approaches across community and home-based settings.
  • Key resources and tools to help older adults remain independent, confident, and safe at home.
