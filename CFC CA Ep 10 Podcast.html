New Podcast Episode on How AI and Technology Are Supporting Seniors Ageing in Place
As Canada’s population continues to age, supporting older adults to live safely, independently, and with dignity at home is an increasingly important priority across the health and ageing services continuum. At the same time, rapid advancements in technology and artificial intelligence (AI) are reshaping how care is delivered, supported, and experienced in the home. From smart devices that assist with daily routines to AI-enabled systems that enhance safety and health monitoring, technology is becoming an integral part of helping older Canadians age in place.
Together, these trends underscore the growing opportunity to thoughtfully integrate AI and smart technology into home environments, not as a replacement for human care, but as a complement that enhances independence, supports safety, and promotes quality of life for older adults and their families.
In this episode of ComForConnections, we examine how smart devices and AI are shaping the future of Ageing in Place in Canada, including their role in safety, health monitoring, daily living support, and how they are redefining independence while supporting dignity, autonomy, and confidence at home.

Learn more about:

  • The role of AI across the care continuum, including whether it functions primarily as a safety net or as an everyday tool that enhances routines, decision-making, engagement, and overall quality of life for older adults.
  • The skills, competencies, and environmental elements needed for success in an AI-supported home, including the evolving role of caregivers and the key features of an ideal AI-enabled home that promote safety, independence, and well-being for older Canadians.
  • Practical considerations for Ageing Services Professionals when leveraging technology and AI to support safe, independent Ageing in Place, including assessment, implementation, training, and ongoing monitoring.
  • Key tools, resources, and partner solutions that help older adults remain confident, mobile, and safe in their home environment.
